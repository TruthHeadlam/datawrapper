The Migrant Crisis On The Field

By: Truth Headlam

Floyd Bennett Field, the latest in communal areas to be contracted by New York state as a base to host a group of asylum seekers that came to the state this year is expected to finally receive its first 500 guests in November.

The state government has received major backlash accompanied by protests for contracting hotels, schools, and communal land used for recreational activities such as Floyd Bennett Field.

Mayor Eric Adams previously condemned the influx of migrants primarily because of the financial stress it would put on the state’s budget but soon after changed his tune and welcomed asylum seekers.

Family Shelter Operation Budget from 2020

Joining the disapproval chain are advocates from the Legal Aid Society and Coalition for the Homeless. The non-profit advocacy groups along with other humanitarian leaders and groups formed NY SANE - New York Shelter for All in Need Equally Coalition. In a joint statement released in October regarding the encampment facilities at Floyd Bennett Field, the groups said:

“Passing out tents as winter approaches is not only a mockery of the City’s legal and moral obligation to provide safe shelter to people without homes, but it will put lives in danger. People freezing to death on the streets is the exact nightmare that the Right to Shelter was designed to prevent.”

According to reports, the Legal Aid Society quickly followed up with a tour of the commissioned area where three large tents sit.
